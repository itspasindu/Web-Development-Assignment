Sri Lanka’s Rich History 🇱🇰

Sri Lanka, once known as Tambapanni, has a history that stretches back over 2,500 years. The name Tambapanni, meaning “copper-colored sands,” was given by Prince Vijaya, the island's first recorded settler. Over time, it was referred to in ancient texts as Lanka, highlighting its sacred and strategic importance in South Asian culture.

During the early centuries, powerful kingdoms such as Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa flourished. Known also as Sinhale or Heladiva, these ancient civilizations left behind impressive legacies—massive stupas, intricate irrigation systems, and sacred Buddhist sites that still draw visitors and pilgrims from around the world.

In the 16th century, Sri Lanka fell under European colonial rule—first by the Portuguese, then the Dutch, and finally the British. Under British governance, the island was renamed Ceylon, and while colonial infrastructure was introduced, strong local resistance movements began to grow in pursuit of independence.

In 1948, Ceylon gained independence, and in 1972, it officially became the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka. The name "Sri Lanka" means "resplendent island" in Sanskrit. Today, the country stands as a beautiful blend of ancient heritage, colonial history, and modern development—a true jewel of the Indian Ocean.
